The Week in Three Points
FOMC: 160bps Fed-ECB Gap Confirmed

The most important structural development of the week. FOMC voted 8-4 to hold and explicitly removed 2026 cut language. Powell: "waiting for backside of energy and tariffs." This widens the Fed-ECB yield gap to 160bps - the highest of the cycle. The ECB is now pricing 3 hikes (June, September, December) while the Fed is on indefinite hold. This rate divergence is the structural driver of EUR strength vs USD over the next 6 months. The FOMC hawkish hold and ECB hawkish pivot are working in the same direction for EUR/USD upside.

USD/JPY ADX H1 reached 63.88 on April 30 - the highest single-timeframe reading this gauge has recorded. This confirms Japan entered the FX market. Officials confirmed market entry after USD/JPY broke toward 160. The intervention drove USD/JPY from the 159 area toward 155-156. The June BoJ hike at 60% odds adds structural JPY buying pressure on top. The intervention ceiling is now confirmed: any USD/JPY approach to 158-160 invites fresh official action.

Combined with CFTC EUR net long +41,324, the structural case for EUR strength is now backed by both positioning and policy momentum. Regime Assessment Post CB Week
Stagflation in Eurozone vs US Divergence

Eurozone data this week paints a clear stagflation picture: inflation accelerating to 3.0% HICP while growth collapses (France GDP 0.0%, Eurozone confidence 93.0 vs 95.2). German retail sales -2.0%. This is rising prices with falling demand. The ECB is forced to hike into weakness - which is why Lagarde's hawkish stance is more complicated than it appears. EUR strength is rate-driven but the Eurozone economy is deteriorating.

US Resilience Confirmed

US capex orders +3.3% vs 0.5% exp. Consumer spending still resilient despite sentiment 49.8 low. Strong enough that Fed can hold indefinitely without recession fears. The divergence: US strong enough to hold rates, Eurozone weak enough that ECB hikes are politically painful. This is the environment where EUR/USD medium-term trajectory depends on which force wins.
