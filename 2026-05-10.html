Framework Adjustment Going Forward
When the macro regime shifts (e.g., USD dump on NFP composition weakness), structural ADX readings need to be deweighted — particularly for pairs where the cross-currency is the dominant force. What killed the trade was the macro regime moving against it with NZ unemployment helping. Adjustment: when NFP / CPI / payrolls deliver a regime-shifting print, the brief should explicitly flag bias-table calls that depend on the prior regime as "regime-conditional" rather than directional.

Real Yields Are The Hinge
The single most important number for both metals into Tuesday is US 10Y real yield. Currently elevated near 2.05-2.15% — the upper end of the multi-month range. Hot CPI pushes it above 2.20% and gold/silver come under pressure regardless of DXY. Soft CPI keeps it capped, and the structural bull continues. Don't watch DXY for metals direction this week — watch real yields.
Oil Premium Deflating
WTI from $108 high to ~$95 close. The Hormuz premium is being repriced lower as Iran-US ceasefire mediation via Pakistan/Qatar progresses. This is gold-neutral, silver-positive: reduced energy inflation pressure caps gold's inflation hedge bid, but lower energy costs improve solar manufacturing margins, supporting silver industrial demand. The cross-effect explains why silver outperformed gold last week (+6.4% vs +2.2%).

US-Iran Ceasefire Holding — Hormuz Reopening Priced In
A fragile three-day ceasefire is holding with mediation routed through Pakistan and Qatar. Iran's response to the US proposal was conveyed through Pakistan (IRNA confirmed). The Qatari Foreign Minister told his Iranian counterpart all parties should respond to mediation efforts. Markets are positioning for a potential reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, which would fully deflate the energy premium. Risk: if talks break down this week, oil retraces toward $108-115, gold tests $4,800, EUR/USD pressures lower on the renewed energy premium. The Trump-Xi summit scheduled for May 14-15 adds a second binary event — markets watching for rare-earth and tech concessions. A failed summit headline is a risk-off catalyst that adds to a hot CPI scenario.
Iran-Israel Sub-Plot Still Active
Despite the broader ceasefire, Israeli airstrikes on Lebanon continue, Iran threats to close Hormuz persist. Maximum alert status remains in effect. The sub-plot is independent of the US-Iran negotiation and represents tail-risk for energy premium re-acceleration.
Russia-Ukraine 3-Day Truce
Trump-announced three-day Russia-Ukraine ceasefire holding. Reduces secondary safe-haven demand. JPY less bid on Ukraine narrative — though intervention dynamics dominate JPY positioning regardless.
Domestic Politics — Powell Out, Warsh In
Powell's Fed Chair term ends Friday May 15. Kevin Warsh is advancing through Senate confirmation with the formal vote scheduled Monday May 11. Warsh is known for hawkish inflation views, skepticism of dot plots and press conferences, and pro-deregulation stance. Markets will begin pricing his approach through any pre-confirmation statements. The structural bid for USD from the Warsh hawkish premium has not yet shown up in DXY (which collapsed to 97.84) — meaning either the market expects Warsh to soften under political pressure, or the broader USD dump on NFP composition + COT collapse is dominant. Watch for any Warsh statements between confirmation and his first FOMC.
