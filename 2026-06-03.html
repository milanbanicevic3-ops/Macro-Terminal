Two Things That Matter Into ECB And NFP

The Regime Tip Stalled On Three Counts
The setups carried into the week were built on the Friday regime tip continuing — soft PCE, Iran de-escalation, weakening dollar. Three things partially reversed it. First, Trump did not sign the MoU and added a new precondition — written Iranian nuclear concessions in a preliminary deal — which extended the negotiation timeline rather than closing it. Iran-related volatility eased, oil softened, but the safe-haven dollar bid that the deal was supposed to unwind has held. Second, US ISM Manufacturing printed 54.5, the strongest reading in months, reinforcing the USD on growth resilience. Third, the same regime that supports a USD bid now also has a new headwind for the high-beta shorts — Australian May CPI accelerated to 4.4% from 4.3%, the upside surprise that the AUD short Thesis Invalidation was sized for. The honest read: the regime is range-bound between Friday's deal-driven softness and this week's data-driven firmness, awaiting ECB Thursday and NFP Friday for resolution.
The Book Is Under Pressure, But No Stops Hit
USD/JPY short at 159.5-159.8 sits roughly 70-130 pips from the 160.50 stop, with H4 ADX firming to NEUTRAL 23.10 against the position. NZD/USD long at ~0.5915 sits closer to the 0.5870 stop, with the H1 momentum that drove Sunday's V.STRONG 61.59 reading having collapsed to NEUTRAL 23.32. XAU/USD long stays gated and never triggered, but the conditions that were converging have substantially receded with gold breaking $4,500. None of the named-event Thesis Invalidations have formally fired — BoJ has not held dovish (June 16-17 still ahead), RBNZ has not surprised dovish, the Iran deal has not collapsed, US PCE has not re-accelerated. The trades remain valid by the framework's letter, but the momentum and price-action evidence that initiated them has weakened materially. The discipline question is whether to manage to stops on the structural cases or close early on the tactical reversal. The answer differs by setup: USD/JPY still has BoJ June 17 as its primary catalyst, so the structural case holds; NZD/USD lost its momentum signal entirely, so the case is materially weaker.

The Iran Reality Check Was Pricing In Quietly
Seven weeks into the truce and nearly two weeks since the draft MoU was agreed, the formalization has not happened. Trump said reports of US-Iran talks ceasing are false, framed Iran as wanting a deal, and expects the deal to extend the truce — positive language. But Trump also told ABC he seeks "written nuclear concessions" in a preliminary deal, which expands what's being negotiated rather than narrowing it, and stretches the timeline. Markets had priced the deal as 60-70% likely on Friday's close; by Wednesday the dollar's weekly recovery shows that pricing has materially eased. The honest read is the market may have been deluded into believing the deal was around the corner, and this week is the reality check — not because the deal has collapsed, but because it has not progressed. Hormuz Strait remains stalled, the energy crisis driver intact, and the safe-haven dollar bid persists despite the broad de-escalation narrative.
US Data Reinforces The Regime Underneath
ISM Manufacturing at 54.5 from 52.3 March is the strongest reading in months — growth resilience reasserting. Neither scenario fits perfectly with the active book's positioning; both threaten the regime tip the setups were built on.
ECB First Hike Since September 2023

German May CPI at -0.2% m/m (vs +0.10% expected) muddies the picture — a single soft regional print, but consistent with the "ECB hawkish but hampered by growth" theme.

Watch for RBA commentary in the coming days; if hawkish, the TI is triggered and the runner closes at breakeven.
