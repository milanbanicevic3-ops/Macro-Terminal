What Worked
The named-event Thesis Invalidation discipline worked exactly as designed — Sunday Setup 1 EUR/USD long thesis killed at Core CPI 0.4% threshold, position exited clean. No fighting price, no rationalizing past the invalidation. The Wednesday flip to EUR/USD short captured the regime change directly: by Friday the pair was -50 pips in profit. The NZD/USD short framework call paid off precisely: Sunday's framework note that "structural ADX needs deweighting when macro regime shifts" translated into the new Wednesday setup, which then saw H1 ADX explode from 21.67 to V.STRONG 50.49 once the post-CPI regime tilted fully. The USD/JPY short setup that didn't trigger on Sunday is now live at the updated intervention zone — macro thesis got stronger (BoJ June 70% priced), entry just took longer to develop.
What Didn't
The XAU/USD long Wednesday thesis revision missed. Sunday's original Thesis Invalidation read: "Real yields above 2.20% removing the real-yield tailwind." That was the correct framing. Wednesday's revision read: "Iran ceasefire suddenly restored AND PPI/Retail confirm CPI was noise." Neither of those Wednesday-revision conditions met, so the position stayed open into Thursday-Friday. What actually killed it was the original Sunday condition triggering — 10Y breaking to 4.51% (highest since June 2025), real yields cleared 2.20% definitively, gold capitulated. The stop at $4,640 hit on schedule. Framework lesson: When tightening a stop on resilience-proven, don't change the Thesis Invalidation language. The original named event was correct. The revision added two conditions ("AND") that were less likely to trigger, which made the thesis appear more durable than it was.
Framework Adjustment Going Forward
Two rules tightened from this week's evidence:

Rule 1 — Thesis Invalidations don't get revised on tightening. When a setup proves resilient on a test (like gold's $9 drop on hot CPI), the response is to tighten the stop, not to revise the Thesis Invalidation framing. The original named event was named for a reason. If the original is broader and the revision is narrower, the revision creates false durability. From now on: stop adjustments are independent of Thesis Invalidation. The Thesis Invalidation is set on initiation and stays fixed for the life of the trade.

Rule 2 — "AND" conditions in Thesis Invalidations are weaker than "OR" conditions. The Wednesday revised gold thesis required both ceasefire restoration and PPI/Retail Sales softening. That's two named events that had to align — lower probability than a single named event. Going forward: Thesis Invalidations should use "OR" between named events, not "AND," unless the trade specifically requires multiple confirmations.
