Three Things That Matter Going Into PCE Week

The COT Extremes Are The Story
Three positioning extremes define the week. JPY net short hit a fresh cycle extreme at -93,905 — specs piled in another 25,251 shorts as the pair ground toward 160, even as ADX went WEAK on every timeframe. That is a spring under maximum tension into BoJ June 17: if the BoJ delivers anything hawkish, the squeeze is violent. CAD net short nearly doubled to -31,231, confirming the USD/CAD long that already had the board's strongest momentum (H4 V.STRONG 44.9). AUD held its record long at +85,644 — the crowded position that keeps the unwind risk live. The cleanest read of the tape: positioning and price structure are aligned, which is why the COT signals and Top Setups reinforce each other this week rather than conflict.
Iran Is The Swing Factor, And It Is Genuinely Two-Sided
Over the weekend, Arabic sources reported the US and Iran reacted positively to an agreement draft, with new Pakistani mediation narrowing differences and Rubio sounding cautiously optimistic. Oil dropped to roughly $92 from $98. But the same weekend brought a CENTCOM "critical stage" blockade statement and an Iranian "highest readiness" declaration — the familiar hope-and-threat whipsaw. The honest assessment is skeptical: the core obstacles (handover of enriched uranium, sanctions removal, and control of Hormuz, where Iran is even weighing a transit toll with Oman that Trump opposes) are exactly the issues that have stalled every prior round. If a firm deal lands and oil keeps falling, it is the regime-flipper — the safe-haven dollar bid unwinds, EUR and AUD rally on risk-on, and the short complex is threatened. The one setup it helps is USD/CAD long, since lower oil pressures the loonie. Weight it as the primary risk to the shorts, not as the base case.
PCE Friday Tests Whether The Regime Can Sustain Itself
Monday is Memorial Day, US closed, so the data compresses into a short week culminating in US Core PCE on Friday May 29 — the Fed's preferred inflation gauge and the resolver of the inflation-regime question that the hawkish FOMC minutes set up. The backdrop still holds: US PMIs show the economy not yet significantly hit by the energy crisis, which strengthens the dollar's safe-haven status, while the growth underneath narrows toward energy-driven consumption. A hot core PCE confirms the regime and supports every USD setup; a soft print, especially with weak consumer components, triggers the EUR/USD Thesis Invalidation, threatens the dollar longs, and is the most likely gold re-entry catalyst above $4,640. Eurozone flash CPI the same day is the other side of the EUR/USD binary. Reduce discretionary size into the PCE-plus-month-end close.

Iran: The Closest Thing To Progress Yet, Against The Loudest Threats
The weekend produced the genuine progress signal the conflict has lacked: Arabic sources reported the US and Iran reacted positively to an agreement draft, new Pakistani mediation narrowed differences, and Rubio sounded cautiously optimistic, with Trump signaling the next few days as critical. Oil fell to roughly $92. But the same window brought a CENTCOM statement that the Iran blockade reached a "critical stage" and an Iranian declaration of highest readiness across all operations — the hope-and-threat pattern at maximum amplitude. The skeptical read: the obstacles that have stalled every prior round remain — handover of enriched uranium, sanctions removal, and control of Hormuz, where Iran is weighing a transit-toll system with Oman that Trump opposes. A firm deal plus sustained oil selling is the single combination that flips the dollar regime — it unwinds the safe-haven bid, lifts EUR and AUD, and threatens the short complex while helping USD/CAD. Weight it as the primary risk to the shorts, not as the base case, and respect that a durable oil break below $100 likely requires Hormuz reopening.
BoJ Coiled, MoF Intervention Spent
USD/JPY sits near 159 with ADX WEAK on all four timeframes and COT shorts at a cycle extreme -93,905. Suspected intervention through recent weeks shook markets without lasting effect — confirming intervention alone cannot reverse the trend while the yield differential dominates. The BoJ June 17 meeting is the catalyst that resolves the coil. A hawkish hike forces the squeeze the positioning is primed for; a dovish hold leaves the carry trade intact. The squeeze-standby is built to catch the former without betting on it in advance.
Warsh Sworn In, Inflation Mandate Explicit
Warsh was sworn in at the White House with Trump present — an unusual venue underscoring the political context. He inherits a Fed facing presidential pressure for lower rates, growing FOMC divisions, and a market pricing hikes rather than cuts, with inflation above target and an energy shock worsening it. Trump emphasized Warsh's independence while making the political line clear: inflation must be stopped. Friday's PCE is the first major data test of whether the restrictive lean the minutes revealed is justified by the data or running ahead of a narrowing economy.
