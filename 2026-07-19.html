Analyst Conclusion
Dominant theme, primary risk, clearest setups

This was the lowest-consensus, lowest-conviction macro week of the series so far. Sources couldn't agree on the basic regime, one likely recycled a four-day-old headline as breaking news, and the honest response was to lean on positioning and technicals rather than force a clean narrative that wasn't there.

The genuinely useful signal this week came from the COT tab and the Momentum Gauge, not the macro commentary. CAD's fresh net-short extreme keeps the USD/CAD technical case intact even with its Thesis Invalidation still formally unresolved from Wednesday. GBP's confirmed two-report short-covering trend, stacked on Friday's structural headwind and a genuine technical consolidation zone (three of four ADX timeframes reading Weak), is now four independent signals pointing against the GBP/USD short without yet triggering the formal TI — the accumulation is worth more attention than any single one of them.

NZD/USD remains the most uncomfortable position on the book. Strong-to-Very-Strong momentum has run against it for three consecutive sessions now, with no fresh positioning data available this week to check the other side of the argument. ECB Thursday is the week's one genuinely high-conviction event and the most likely candidate to reset the tone for everything above it.
