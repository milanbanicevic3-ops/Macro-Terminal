The Week In One Read

The Regime Tipped On Two Parallel Forces
For three weeks the brief had been calling a USD-bid regime resting on the inflation print plus the Iran safe-haven premium, while flagging the same two-sided risk: a soft PCE plus a confirmed Iran de-escalation as the regime-flipper. Both arrived on the same Friday. Core PCE printed +0.2% month-on-month versus +0.3% expected — not a material undershoot, but enough to take the edge off the most hawkish Fed scenarios — while Treasury Secretary Bessent moved publicly to endorse a Hormuz transit-toll arrangement and Iranian state media published initial details of a Memorandum of Understanding covering US military withdrawal from Iran's vicinity, a Navy lifting of the Hormuz blockade, and Iranian commitments on commercial transit numbers. Oil collapsed from roughly $103 on Wednesday to $89.32 by Friday close — a clean market vote that the deal is being priced as credible. The dollar broadly weakened, gold's re-entry conditions started to converge, and the JPY positioning extreme suddenly had its catalyst forming early.
The Framework Did Its Job, And The Book Is Ready For The Next Phase
The USD/CAD long was the featured trade for a reason — H4 ADX V.STRONG, COT short doubled, momentum and positioning aligned — and trailing its stop up to 1.3740 on Wednesday is what turned a Friday selloff into a logged win rather than a giveback. The USD/JPY standby refused to feature a direction in the intervention zone, which is what saved the book from fighting the grind to 160 for a third straight week. The NZD stand-aside Wednesday avoided buying the wrong side of a hawkish-RBNZ rally. The EUR and AUD shorts scratched without their stops hit and without their Thesis Invalidations fully triggering — honest losses of momentum, not framework failures. For next week, the position is to ride the regime tip via three setups that are now catalyst-converging rather than catalyst-waiting — USD/JPY short, XAU/USD long, NZD/USD long — while staying alert to the central caveat: the accord is a draft, not a signature.

What Worked
The risk-management decisions Wednesday were what made the week work. Trailing the USD/CAD long stop up to 1.3740 turned the trade from "fighting the Friday selloff" into a logged win — the H4 close below 1.3740 took it out cleanly, locking roughly 30 pips from the 1.3771 entry before the broad USD weakness compressed the move. The standing-aside discipline on USD/JPY was equally important. Featuring a directional short there for two prior weeks had cost; reclassifying it to a squeeze-standby gated on the BoJ catalyst stopped the bleeding, and this week the catalyst started to form (Ueda hawkish, ~75% June hike priced) — precisely the setup the standby was built for. The NZD short stand-aside Wednesday was the framework's clearest call — a positioning signal without a fundamental tailwind is not a trade — and the Friday rally proved it.
What Didn't, And Why It's Not A Failure
The EUR and AUD shorts both scratched into the regime flip without stops hit and without their Thesis Invalidations fully triggering. EUR's TI was set as "EZ flash CPI hot OR US PCE undershoots materially" — the +0.2% miss on monthly PCE was mild, not material, so the TI registered only a partial signal. AUD's TI was "China stimulus OR RBA signals a hike" — neither happened. What killed the tactical performance was the Friday risk-on dollar selloff, which is the named risk the Sunday brief flagged as the primary threat to the short complex. The fact that the trade scratched rather than stopped is the framework absorbing exactly the scenario it was warned about. The honest takeaway: the named-event Thesis Invalidations behaved correctly — they did not false-trigger, they did not hold beyond their justification — but they were calibrated for the inflation-regime continuation, and a regime tip is a different category of event. Next week's setups carry catalyst-aware Thesis Invalidations built for the new regime, not the old.
Framework Note Carried Forward
Three principles from this week to internalize. First, trailing stops on featured trades when the catalyst risk approaches is what converts conviction into logged P&L. The USD/CAD long had a justified Thesis Invalidation tied to Iran de-escalation; tightening protection ahead of the Friday print was the operational expression of taking that named risk seriously. Second, standby setups are protections, not deferrals. The USD/JPY standby refused to be wrong directly for a third week; if June 16-17 delivers, the squeeze positioning catches the move from the right side. Third, when the regime tips, the right move is to rotate to setups built around the new catalyst rather than to double the existing book. The EUR and AUD shorts get stood aside or held passively; the USD/JPY short, XAU/USD long, and NZD/USD long activate. Different regimes need different trades.
