Two Things That Define The Week

The Institutional Hawkish Shift Is The Week's Defining Event
Inside five trading days two G10 central banks moved their formal positions hawkish via official documents. The RBA's June 4 Senate statement added language on inflation pass-through second-round effects and underlying inflation remaining above target until mid-2027, removed the explicit "space to see how the conflict plays out" patience language, and shifted forward guidance from "a bit restrictive" to "well placed to respond... Board will do what it considers necessary" — the most aggressive verbal posture Bullock has taken this cycle. The BoJ's June 3 Comprehensive Policy Review added explicit acknowledgment of "secondary spillover effects" raising the risk of an "upward deviation in underlying inflation", added new vigilance language prioritizing inflation control, and replaced "large-scale monetary easing framework" references with normalized policy execution language. This is institutional positioning, not data-driven repricing — two central banks moving their official stance via formal documents in the same week represents a different category of signal than CPI prints. For the book, RBA's shift triggered Setup 4's Thesis Invalidation (clean close at breakeven), BoJ's shift dramatically reinforced Setup 1's structural case ahead of the June 16-17 meeting.
The Iran De-Escalation Reversed, But Not Catastrophically
Friday May 29's regime tip narrative survived the first weekend stress test — Trump kept the MoU formalization door open, Bessent endorsed transit-toll diplomacy. By midweek the picture had deteriorated meaningfully. Iran formally withdrew from US negotiations, ceased message exchanges, attacked US targets in Kuwait and Bahrain. US forces struck the Iranian island of Qeshm near Hormuz — the largest exchange of fire since the April truce. Japanese Finance Minister Katayama referenced "the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz" in his market-intervention comments, confirming that the binding energy-crisis constraint remains fully active. What kept the situation contained: Israel-Lebanon ceasefire deal in place cushioning the regional escalation, Trump publicly remaining optimistic about a deal by weekend, the US House passing a symbolic Iran-war resolution signaling inflation concern from elected officials. Oil whipsawed but settled at $78 today, down 1% on session, pulling back from week highs. The deal narrative is now stress-tested but alive, not collapsed. Markets digested NFP into the close.
NFP Resolved Strong — USD Bid Reasserted
The May employment release printed strong across the labor-market spectrum, with the headline beating consensus and the wage and unemployment legs aligning to support the USD. RBA — Bullock Senate Statement Delivers The Hawkish Pivot
Bullock's opening statement to the Senate Economics Legislation Committee on June 4 contained the most consequential language shift of the year for AUD positioning. The Board has moved from a wait-and-see posture to active data-dependent tightening bias inside a single quarter. The detail of what changed matters:

Added — second-round inflation pass-through: "Tentative signs that higher fuel-related costs may have been passed through... And there are indications that there are likely to be second-round effects." This confirms that structural, broad-based inflation pass-through is materializing in real time rather than remaining a theoretical threat in forecasts.

Added — extended timeline for restrictive policy: "Underlying inflation remains above the target range until mid-2027." This explicitly extends the timeline for elevated inflation, signaling that restrictive policy will need to stay in place longer than previously emphasized in February estimates.

Removed — the patience language: "This gives the Board space to see how the conflict plays out" was dropped. The deletion of explicit "space to wait" framing signals increasing urgency to act if data remains hot. The April hike already happened (cash rate increased per the statement); the question is now sequencing of additional hikes, not whether to start.

Wording shift — from "a bit" to "do what it considers necessary": The February language describing the stance as "a bit restrictive" was upgraded to "monetary policy is well placed to respond to developments" with the Board "prepared to do what it considers necessary." The softening qualifier "a bit" was dropped, and the resolve language tightened materially.

For the book this is the explicit hike-signaling Setup 4's Thesis Invalidation was sized for. The runner closes at breakeven per the framework. The named-event TI structure worked exactly as designed: the institutional catalyst, not the price action or the COT positioning, was the resolver.
BoJ — Comprehensive Policy Review Hawkish Pre-Positioning
The June 3 Comprehensive Policy Review delivered the institutional hawkish shift the USD/JPY squeeze structure had been waiting for — and notably, it arrived ahead of the formal June 16-17 meeting rather than at it. This is pre-positioning that strengthens, not undermines, the Setup 1 structural case.

Added — structural exit from deflation acknowledged: "Japan is currently in a situation in which the secondary spillover effects... are more likely to lead to an upward deviation in underlying inflation." This is the BoJ formally recognizing that Japan's structural inflation backdrop has shifted in a way that makes the economy highly sensitive to upside inflation shocks.

Added — explicit vigilance priority on inflation: "The Bank... should be more vigilant about the risk of a significant upward deviation in inflation materializing." This explicitly prioritizes inflation control over economic growth protections in the policy mandate triage — the language a central bank uses when it is preparing to act.

Removed — emergency easing framework references: The "large-scale monetary easing framework" language that anchored prior BoJ communications has been replaced entirely with normalized policy execution language. The framework is no longer "easing"; it is now "normalizing."

For the book, this dramatically reinforces Setup 1. The TI ("BoJ June 16-17 holds fully dovish with no hawkish guidance") was sized to protect against the scenario where the institutional catalyst fails to deliver. The June 3 review delivers the institutional catalyst ahead of schedule. Per Japanese OIS, 75% of a June 16 hike is now priced. The Finance Minister referenced "the effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz" in his intervention-watch commentary — intervention risk and BoJ tightening can coexist, and likely will. The Setup 1 structural case is the cleanest catalyst-positioning alignment of any active book entry.

The "insurance hike" framing limits the EUR upside from the rate-differential channel because the market reads it as discrete rather than the first of multiple. EZ flash CPI at 3.0% headline with core at 2.2% supports a cautious cycle stance; German May preliminary CPI at -0.2% m/m soft adds further reason for the ECB to flag growth concerns alongside the hike. The stand-aside discipline through Thursday was correct; the post-decision read confirms that EUR remains directionless without a clearer ECB cycle signal.
