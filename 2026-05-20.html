UK CPI: Sticky Number, Absent Mechanism
UK core CPI printed 4.2%, above the 4.0% line. On its face that is GBP-supportive — persistent price pressure discourages aggressive BoE cuts. But the mechanism that would actually rescue cable did not appear: BoE hike pricing did not revive. Markets continue to price BoE cuts through 2026, just at a slower pace. Meanwhile UK unemployment rose to roughly 4.9-5.0% and wages ex-bonus continued slowing — softer labor that argues for cuts, not hikes. Layered on top is the political and fiscal stress: Starmer fighting to hold his position, a possible left-leaning successor (Burnham or Streeting), and 30Y gilt yields above 5% drawing explicit Liz Truss comparisons. The net is that GBP/USD stays bearish on the political and fiscal story, not on a CPI-driven BoE bid that never materialized. Setup 5 stays alive because the named-event Thesis Invalidation ("sticky core reviving BoE hike pricing") did not trigger — the number hit but the mechanism did not.

The Iran Overlay Stays Unresolved
Two and a half months into the conflict, no comprehensive deal. Trump postponed a planned Iran strike after requests from Saudi, Qatar, and UAE leaders, reviving peace hopes — but a rumored partial oil-sanctions removal proved incorrect and the brief risk-on move reversed. Oil holds above $100; a deal would not drop oil quickly because supply routes take months to normalize. The standing risk: Trump may attempt another de-escalation headline to calm markets ahead of midterm season and the summer driving period when expensive fuel becomes a political liability. A genuine de-escalation plus oil selling is the combination that would flip the USD lower and reopen EUR/USD and gold longs — but until both appear together, the dollar-strength regime holds.
