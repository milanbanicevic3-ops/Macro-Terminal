Regime

This is a genuine risk-off session driven by a real, verifiable escalation, not a repeat of the stale-headline issue from two weeks ago. The Houthi movement has declared a naval blockade on Saudi Arabia through the Bab el-Mandeb strait, a chokepoint carrying roughly 12% of world trade, with immediate effect. Two US soldiers were killed as Iranian attacks on US regional bases intensify, with air raid sirens active across Gulf states.

Oil is the clearest single read on how seriously markets are taking this. Crude is up roughly 25% from this month's lows, with drone strikes also disrupting loading at a Caspian pipeline terminal, adding a second supply-side channel beyond Hormuz and Bab el-Mandeb.

Bond markets are telling a story equities haven't fully priced yet. The 10-year Treasury yield is heading back toward its earlier war-driven highs even after last week's cooler CPI print — a signal that markets are pricing renewed inflation risk and continued escalation over a durable ceasefire, regardless of what either side says officially about the status of the existing agreement.

Central bank stances
Fed

Hawkish hold intact at the upper 3.75% range. Markets price a hike later in 2026 as effectively certain, but give only about 15% odds to a hike as soon as this month's meeting. A resilient consumer, per recent BLS and bank-institute data, supports a longer pause rather than urgent action either direction — if the Fed stays on hold while the economy stays resilient, the dollar can keep gaining even without a hike, simply because other economies are slowing faster.

ECB

Decision Thursday, and the odds of a hawkish surprise have nearly disappeared. July 1's Eurozone inflation report effectively removed 2026 hike expectations from the table. The renewed Middle East and Russia-Ukraine escalation has revived some inflation concern, and markets now price a September hike as nearly certain — but only around 15% odds for a move this week. A hawkish tone without a hike could still help EUR modestly; the more likely balanced outcome probably won't.

BoJ

No acceleration signal in Japanese inflation, and no urgency shift from the BoJ. USD/JPY continues oscillating above 162 without clear direction; volatility has fallen to its lowest level since the start of the year, which may reduce the near-term urgency for intervention. PM Takaichi continues favoring fiscal stimulus to lift domestic growth and prices.

BoE

A data-heavy week with UK CPI Wednesday as the most likely market mover, more than the rest of the calendar combined. Burnham formally took office July 20 as the new UK PM; confirmation that Mahmood becomes Chancellor has calmed gilt holders, but real uncertainty remains around how his public investment plans get funded.

CAD / AUD / NZD

All three have benefited from broad dollar softness, and now increasingly from the oil move directly. Since last week's BoC decision left no hawkish impression, today's Canadian CPI could pause CAD's advance temporarily, but renewed inflation from higher oil should continue supporting it as a commodity currency into any future tightening repricing. Strong Australian employment data plus signals from the upcoming Chinese Politburo meeting could extend AUD's advance; NZD continues benefiting from shifting rate expectations and broad commodity-currency demand.

Currency selection under a strong-dollar view

Worth a structural note on which pairs actually track the dollar cleanly right now. Most currencies have moved with the dollar closely for years — JPY and CHF are the persistent exceptions, for structural reasons unrelated to the current cycle: JPY stays weak on Japan's stimulative monetary and fiscal mix, while CHF carries constant safe-haven demand independent of the dollar's own direction. With the dollar sitting at historically elevated levels against most currencies, some central banks are now actively resisting further depreciation of their own currency against it — South Korea's intervention to defend the won against continued weakness is the clearest recent example. The practical read-through: EUR, GBP, and CHF currently look like the cleanest pairs for expressing a dollar-strength view, since they track the dollar directly rather than fighting a structural distortion the way JPY does.

Earnings season

Earnings season is accelerating, with tech names dominating attention this week. Alphabet and Tesla report Wednesday after the US close, Intel Thursday. Thursday's equity pullback was a reminder of ongoing nervousness about whether massive announced AI infrastructure spending will actually translate into profitability. A run of strong results alongside fresh AI investment commitments could be the deciding factor for the Nasdaq 100, which has lagged the broader market through July.
