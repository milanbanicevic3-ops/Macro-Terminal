Framework Rules Tightened This Week
Two rules formalized from the Friday brief, both confirmed by this week's evidence:

Rule 1 — Thesis Invalidations are fixed at initiation. Stop adjustments are independent. When a setup proves resilient on a test, the response is to tighten the stop, not revise the Thesis Invalidation framing. The Wednesday gold thesis revision is the textbook negative example: revising to "ceasefire restored AND data softening" added an AND that made the thesis appear more durable than reality. The original "real yields above 2.20%" was correct — it triggered, the stop hit, the position closed appropriately.

Rule 2 — "OR" between named events is stronger than "AND". Multiple named events in OR configuration mean any one triggers exit. AND configuration requires all conditions, which lowers the trigger probability and creates false durability. Setup 3 NZD/USD short this week uses OR ("RBNZ pivots OR NZ employment improves OR US data reversal") — that's the correct structure.
