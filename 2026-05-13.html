What Actually Happened
Headline CPI accelerated to 3.8% YoY from 3.3% prior — a 50bp m/m jump driven by the energy shock from Hormuz disruptions filtering through to retail prices. The headline beat consensus by 10bp. The market reaction wasn't to the headline. The market reaction was to Core m/m at 0.4% vs 0.3% expected — the spillover of energy inflation into non-energy prices that the Fed has been watching for. Core m/m at 0.4% sustained for two more months is a 4.8% annualized run rate, well above the Fed's 2% target. That's what removed all easing expectations and put 17bp of hikes back on the table through April 2027. The thesis Powell named at the April 29 FOMC — "we'd want to see the backside of energy and tariffs" — just got harder to defend. CPI Tuesday was the binary event flagged on Sunday. The print landed exactly at the named threshold.
The Fed Path Re-priced
Before CPI: market priced 5bp of total Fed easing through 2026, with a possibility of one cut by year-end. After CPI: 17bp of hikes priced through April 2027, nearly 70% probability of a 25bp hike at one of the upcoming meetings. That's a complete inversion of the easing-bias narrative that drove the post-NFP USD weakness. Combined with Warsh's confirmed hawkish lean ahead of his Friday May 15 assumption of the Chair, the structural USD bid has its institutional anchor back.
ECB Tries To Match But Differential Holds
ECB news this week was unusually hawkish for the central bank. Nagel confirmed two hikes in the baseline scenario. Money markets now price three ECB hikes for 2026. Kocher warned a rate move is "unavoidable unless energy improves rapidly." ZEW May German economic sentiment beat at -10.2 vs -19.8 expected. If this had landed in a vacuum, EUR/USD would have rallied through 1.18. Instead the rate differential held in USD's favor because the Fed pivot from cuts to hikes is the larger move. EUR has its own story; USD has the bigger story.
JPY Squeeze On Pause, Not Cancelled
USD/JPY went from 156.70 Friday to 157.70 Wednesday despite the BoJ June hike pricing rising from 60% to 70%. The price action and the macro thesis diverged. The reconciliation: the post-CPI USD bid overwhelmed the structural JPY squeeze on a short-term basis, but did not invalidate it. Bessent-Katayama coordination was confirmed this week — the US Treasury Secretary is in Japan to coordinate on yen movements. Tuesday morning's brief JPY spike that quickly faded was likely a "rate check" or minor intervention. The full intervention sits at 158-160. The setup is the same as Sunday with one update: the entry zone moves higher because the pair didn't crack 155 this week and is now trading near intervention zone. What This Tested About The Framework
Three setups, three different outcomes. Setup 1 closed cleanly per the discipline rule — named thesis invalidation triggered, position exited. The rule worked exactly as designed: no fighting price action, no rationalizing past the invalidation. Setup 2 illustrates the difference between thesis and timing. This is the trade-off built into using key levels as triggers rather than market orders. Setup 3 illustrates resilience as information.
