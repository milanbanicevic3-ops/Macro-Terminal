Central bank stances
Fed
Hawkish hold at 3.50–3.75%. Minutes today are the read-through for whether the June NFP miss has genuinely shifted internal Committee thinking, or whether the checkpoint-not-reversal framing from last week still holds.
ECB
Steady, watchful. ECB Minutes drop Thursday from the meeting that delivered a 25bp hike and lifted the deposit rate to 2.25% with raised inflation projections — but officials tied future moves explicitly to incoming data, especially energy. With oil back near pre-conflict levels, the market only prices ~30% odds of a follow-up hike this month, down from a more confident read right after that meeting.
BoJ
Structurally passive despite the hike to ~1.00% already delivered. USD/JPY has nearly erased last week's pullback and is pressing back toward 162 as Japanese authorities have quietly dropped their practice of pre-announcing intervention warnings — a tactical shift the market has read as permissive rather than deterrent, with shorts on JPY continuing to build on loose fiscal policy under PM Takaichi.
BoE
Steady, data-dependent hold. Sterling is quietly outperforming both EUR and USD into the July 20 handover to incoming PM Burnham — EUR/GBP sits at a one-year low despite ECB hawkishness, which says more about GBP strength than EUR weakness.
RBNZ
Hiked 25bp today, confirmed. Vote at the prior meeting was a genuine 3-3 split with the Governor's tie-break holding rates, but forward guidance was explicitly more hawkish than before — markets had priced roughly 80% odds of today's hike plus two more of the same size baked in through year-end. A hike alone may not be enough to sustain NZD strength if the accompanying statement doesn't validate that further-tightening path.
RBA / SNB / BoC
RBA steady, neutral-to-hawkish undertone. SNB unchanged, still favoring a weaker franc. BoC neutral; Wednesday's Ivey PMI and Friday's employment print are the next real signal on whether CAD's oversold positioning gets any relief.
Geopolitical & political
Hormuz traffic continues normalizing — roughly 160 vessels transited last week despite a brief pause in US-Iran talks tied to mourning after a former Iranian leader's funeral. OPEC+ added a further 188,000 bpd from August, though actual production catching up to the new quota will take time, capping the near-term bearish oil impulse. Separately, speculation is building that ECB's Lagarde could step down before her term ends to pursue France's 2027 presidential race — if real, she'd need to formally enter the race before year-end, which would make the September ECB meeting one of her last. Not a near-term market mover, but worth tracking as a 2H26 story.

✗
Proprietary framework view runs against the source consensus on gold. The desk's own analytical framework has stayed a seller into rallies since the last major top, on the view that the current pullback most likely needs months (potentially longer) of consolidation before a genuine bottom forms — consistent with every prior cycle in this pattern, where the crowd tried to call bottoms mid-decline and got run over. The mechanism matters here: gold has rallied hard specifically when the Fed cuts into higher inflation (a credibility hedge). If Warsh succeeds in restoring Fed credibility and any future cuts come with inflation genuinely falling rather than rising, that mechanism reverses and gold loses its primary support, regardless of how "safe-haven" the broader narrative still sounds. Worth weighing this against the source consensus above rather than treating XAUUSD as a simple risk-off/risk-on trade this week.

Analyst Conclusion
Dominant theme, primary risk, clearest setups
The dominant theme today is a hawkish central bank actually delivering on its guidance — RBNZ's confirmed 25bp hike stands out precisely because most of the week's other narratives (Fed, ECB) are stuck in wait-and-see mode ahead of this afternoon's FOMC Minutes. That confirmation also doubled as a useful stress test of the source discipline itself: a majority of this session's models got the actual RBNZ outcome wrong in opposite directions, which is exactly the scenario the multi-source, majority-vote framework exists to catch rather than launder into the brief. The primary risk to the session is the 12:15–18:00 GMT window — ADP, ISM Services, and FOMC Minutes stacked back to back, with the Minutes doubling as the live test of the GBP/USD Thesis Invalidation. The clearest setups remain USD/CAD long (technically unchanged, conviction flagged as the daily bias table turns against it) and GBP/USD short (upgraded on confirmed momentum, but paused into the Minutes). AUD/USD is worth watching closely — momentum has genuinely rebuilt since the short was closed — but stays as color until a real Thesis Invalidation is defined, not promoted on the strength of an ADX reading alone.
