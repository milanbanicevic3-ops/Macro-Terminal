Regime
The June NFP miss (+57K vs +110K expected, with the household survey down 700K in June and 1.3M since the start of 2025) is the single dominant input this week. It has pulled DXY off its highs toward the 100.00–100.85 zone and re-opened the case for the Fed to move on rate cuts sooner than the market was pricing pre-print. Equities and commodities are bid on the back of it — classic soft-landing repricing, not a growth scare yet. A clear majority of source models converge on this read independently.
✗
Source conflict caught and discarded: One source's macro read is internally stale — it describes an "80% probability of a 25bp hike by December" under a hawkish Warsh Fed, which is the pre-NFP narrative from earlier in the week, not a response to the print. Every other source, and the print itself, point the other way. That source's conclusion is not used anywhere in this brief.
Central bank stances
Fed
On hold at 3.50–3.75%. NFP miss has meaningfully cooled hike probability and reopened a cut discussion for late Q3/Q4. FOMC Minutes (Wed) are the next real test of how much the Committee itself has shifted.
ECB
Steady, balanced-to-slightly-cautious. Sticky services inflation keeps Lagarde from leaning dovish, but growth data (French GDP flat, German retail sales -2.0%) is deteriorating under the surface.
BoJ
Confirmed spot intervention on USD/JPY already fired. Structurally hawkish-leaning under pressure to defend the yen, but no fresh policy signal this week — watching for stabilization near 161.50–162.00 before treating any bounce as more than defense.
BoE
Data-dependent hold. Bailey has flagged UK inflation could still run toward 3.0–3.2% later this year, which is the main pillar keeping GBP bid against a broadly softer dollar.
RBA / RBNZ
RBA leans hawkish on resilient employment and commodity exposure; RBNZ decision lands Wednesday and is a binary vol event for NZD — base case dovish hold, but not a high-conviction call.
SNB / BoC
SNB dovish, favoring a weaker franc for exporters. BoC neutral, tracking US trade-partner weakness and soft crude directly through CAD.
Geopolitical
Risk tone easing on two fronts: Hormuz shipping activity resuming (Qatar confirming maritime activity resumption, vessel transits through the Strait recovering) and quiet progress on indirect US-Iran contact. OPEC+ met over the weekend and is reportedly discussing raising quotas amid the de-escalation — net effect is a lid on oil and one less inflation-side argument for the Fed to stay restrictive. NATO's pledge of 70bn EUR in further Ukraine military aid for 2026 keeps a low, non-market-moving hum of European risk in the background, not a front-page driver this week.
◆
Warsh's public tone at Sintra alongside Lagarde/Bailey/Macklem has been read across sources as broadly softer than his prior guidance. Per the standing house view: bond yields have not confirmed a dovish regime change. This is a checkpoint, not a reversal — every bullish-USD-weakness call in this brief should be read with that ceiling in mind.

Analyst Conclusion
Dominant theme, primary risk, clearest setups
The dominant theme this week is the market unwinding a chunk of its US dollar premium after a badly missed NFP print, with equities and metals bid on the back of a softer Fed-cut runway. That said, this is a checkpoint, not a confirmed regime change — bond yields haven't moved enough yet to say the dovish pivot is real, and Warsh's softer Sintra tone is talk until the FOMC Minutes back it up. The primary risk to the whole tape is Thursday's US Core CPI: an upside surprise collides directly with the newly-formed soft-landing consensus and would unwind a lot of this week's dollar softness fast. The clearest structural setups remain GBP/USD short (positioning + technical, not the daily consensus) and USD/CAD long (technically intact, macro conviction downgraded) — both sit on named, falsifiable Thesis Invalidations rather than price levels. AUD/USD and USD/JPY stay as color, not positions, until each produces a genuinely fresh signal rather than a stale thesis wearing new price levels.
