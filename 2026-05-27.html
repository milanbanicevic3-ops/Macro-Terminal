Two Things That Matter Into Friday

PCE Friday With Risks Skewed Up
US Core PCE for April — the Fed's preferred inflation gauge — lands Friday May 29 with personal income and spending, after the Q1 GDP second estimate Thursday. Given the hotter-than-expected CPI and PPI prints for the month, the risks to PCE are skewed to the upside. A further consideration: even if oil does not keep rising, the annual inflation rate is being compared against much lower prices a year ago, so base effects can keep the headline elevated regardless. A hot core PCE confirms the regime that the hawkish FOMC minutes anchored, supports every USD setup, and keeps the rate-hike repricing on track — the market already prices a 25bp hike fully by March 2027 with roughly 70% probability this year. A soft print, especially with weak personal-spending components, triggers the EUR/USD Thesis Invalidation and is the most likely gold re-entry catalyst. Reduce size into the print, compounded by month-end rebalancing the same day.
The USD/JPY Squeeze Is Forming Before The BoJ
The squeeze-standby was built to trigger on the BoJ June 16-17 meeting, but the catalyst is starting to form early. Ueda's hawkish commentary this week lifted the yen, USD/JPY H1 ADX jumped to STRONG 30.91, and the OIS market now prices roughly a 75% probability of a June hike with another nearly fully priced by year-end. Tokyo CPI for May, due with industrial production and employment, is the next input — an acceleration raises the hike odds further. The positioning behind this is the cycle-extreme COT short of -93,905 from Sunday's read. The setup remains a standby because the pair is still grinding in the 158-160 intervention zone where FinMin Katayama amplifies intervention rhetoric — but the distance between standby and trigger is closing. If the BoJ delivers, the squeeze off that positioning extreme should be sharp. The trigger stays an H4 close below 157.00 after the catalyst, not before.

Two Hawkish Central Banks Cut Against Two Short Signals
The week's central-bank news ran against two of the lower-conviction bearish positions. The RBNZ delivered a hawkish hold — restrictive rates, readiness to act "decisively" if inflation pressures build, rising one- and two-year inflation expectations, and a market now pricing roughly three hikes by year-end with July at 80%. That is NZD-supportive and undermines the COT-derived NZD short, which is now best stood aside. BoJ Ueda's hawkish commentary lifted the yen and pushed June hike odds to ~75% — which is the opposite of a problem for the book, because it advances the USD/JPY squeeze-standby toward its trigger. The distinction matters: the RBNZ hawkishness kills a low-conviction signal, while the BoJ hawkishness powers a high-conviction standby that was specifically waiting for it.
AU CPI Softened, AUD Short Intact
Australian CPI for April fell to 3.7% year-on-year, below the 3.8% expected and down from the prior 4.6% in March. The downside surprise cools the aggressive RBA-hike repricing that was the main risk to the AUD short — Setup 3's Thesis Invalidation ("RBA explicitly signals a hike") did not trigger; if anything the soft print reinforces the short. The pair is ranging on H4 (ADX WEAK 11.36) but the weekly structure holds (W1 STRONG 35.56). Sell bounces remains the approach.
Europe Weak, ECB Hawkish-But-Hampered
Eurozone composite PMI fell deeper into contraction at 47.5 from 48.8, Q1 GDP missed at 0.1%, and German consumer confidence hit its lowest since February 2023. The ECB is expected to hike with another 40bp possible after, but the eurozone is more exposed to the energy crisis and Middle East conflict than the US — so the hawkish ECB stance is hampered by deteriorating growth. The EUR/USD short rests on US strength and EZ growth weakness outweighing ECB hawkishness. Friday's German, French, and Italian flash CPI is the first read and the two-sided binary alongside US PCE.
Iran Re-Escalated, But Oil Stayed Capped
The weekend's agreement-draft optimism faded fast. The US carried out new strikes on Iran even as negotiations continue, and Iran's foreign ministry vowed the nation "will respond to defend itself." Oil rallied toward $105 — then pulled back, following the same pattern as the last several times it touched that level. The working read is that the administration has incentive to keep oil capped ahead of the summer driving season, and that a larger escalation is more likely after the midterms than before. For the book, the net is status-quo-supportive: no de-escalation means the safe-haven dollar bid holds, which supports the short complex. The one nuance for USD/CAD: a rallying oil is a terms-of-trade headwind for the long, but CAD has kept underperforming anyway because the broad dollar bid and the doubled CAD short dominate — the trade is working despite the oil tailwind for the loonie.

Iran: New Strikes, Negotiations Continue, Oil Capped
The weekend's agreement-draft optimism faded as the US carried out new strikes on Iran while negotiations continued in parallel, and Iran's foreign ministry vowed the nation "will respond to defend itself." Oil rallied toward $105 then pulled back — the same rejection pattern seen the last several times crude touched that level. For the FX complex, the net is status-quo: no de-escalation means the safe-haven dollar bid persists, supporting the short complex. The one cross-current is USD/CAD, where the oil rally is a terms-of-trade tailwind for the loonie — but the trade has worked anyway because the broad dollar bid and the doubled CAD short outweigh it. A durable oil break below $100 likely still requires Hormuz fully reopening.
BoJ Hike Case Building Toward June 16-17

Tokyo CPI for May, with industrial production and employment, is the next input. The caveat from the tape: the BoJ will have to meet these expectations for any intervention to have the desired effect and prevent further speculative yen weakness. The USD/JPY squeeze-standby is positioned for exactly this — a cycle-extreme COT short waiting for the BoJ to validate the hawkish pricing.
Two Hawkish Holds: RBNZ Now, ECB Ahead
The RBNZ's hawkish hold (three hikes priced, July at 80%) re-rated the NZD higher and removed it from the short list. The ECB is expected to hike at its next meeting with up to 40bp more after — but a eurozone composite PMI at 47.5 and a Q1 GDP miss show the hawkish stance is hampered by deteriorating growth and energy exposure. The divergence theme holds: the dollar's strength rests less on an aggressive Fed than on every other major economy being more exposed to the energy shock — which is why Friday's PCE matters as the test of whether the US data can keep validating the bid.
