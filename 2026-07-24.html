Regime

A genuinely mixed session: resilient US data against a building geopolitical risk that hasn't fully landed yet. US initial jobless claims printed at their lowest level since 1969 — a very strong labor market signal. At the same time, the Middle East conflict shows no sign of settling, with the Bab el-Mandeb strait increasingly discussed as the next flashpoint after Hormuz.

The chokepoint story deserves close attention over the weekend specifically. Saudi Arabian vessels have already come under attack and are rerouting via the Cape of Good Hope rather than risk the Red Sea corridor, while Chinese-flagged shipping continues passing unimpeded. If the pattern extends to targeting vessels tied to any US partner more broadly, oil could push cleanly above $100 a barrel — a real, credible scenario flagged directly rather than a worst-case tail risk.

The yen's decline continues without any concrete policy response. USD/JPY hit a fresh 40-year low for the yen on Tuesday. The absence of intervention so far suggests Japanese authorities aren't treating the pace of the move as urgent, at least not yet.

Central bank stances
Fed

Hawkish hold intact, September back in focus as the likely timing for the next move. The dollar has had a solid week against the basket on the back of that repricing, reinforced by today's very strong jobless claims print.

ECB

Held at 2.25% Thursday, as roughly 95% of priced probability expected. September is now priced at over 85% odds for a follow-up hike given the renewed conflict. The read on EUR either way is unflattering: a hike would further slow an already-weak Eurozone growth picture, while a hold read as complacent about import-inflation risk doesn't help either. Today's Eurozone flash PMIs are the nearest fresh test of that growth concern directly.

BoE

Sterling continues weakening on genuine domestic political and fiscal concerns, not just broad dollar strength. Investors remain unconvinced by PM Burnham's promise to hold existing fiscal commitments; a newly announced business-rate cut for pubs, clubs, and music venues raised fresh concern since it's funded from still-unsecured sources. Yesterday's UK inflation report also reduced near-term BoE hike expectations, removing one of the pillars that had been supporting GBP.

BoJ / RBA / RBNZ / BoC

No fresh signals from any of the four today. Next week's calendar is unusually loaded for all of them — FOMC and BoJ both meet, alongside US Non-Farm Payrolls, making this week's relative calm likely temporary.

Currency selection under the ongoing dollar-strength view

The desk's own framework from earlier in the week continues to hold up in practice. EUR, GBP, CHF, and JPY remain the preferred legs against the dollar, each with a distinct, currently-playing-out reason: GBP on genuine domestic political turmoil, CHF on neutral data and softer gold reducing safe-haven demand, JPY on PM Takaichi's continued stimulative bias, and EUR on a genuinely gloomy Eurozone growth outlook regardless of what the ECB does with rates.

Analyst Conclusion
Dominant theme, primary risk, clearest setups

This week closes with a genuine split between resilient US data and a geopolitical backdrop that keeps quietly worsening. Record-low jobless claims and a solid dollar week sit alongside a chokepoint conflict where Saudi shipping is already rerouting around Africa and a credible scenario exists for oil to push past $100 if the pattern spreads to broader US-partner shipping.

The two active shorts moved in opposite directions from the two active USD-long-adjacent themes this week, and both moves were earned rather than assumed. GBP/USD earns a genuine upgrade to Medium on converging technical, fundamental, and fresh domestic political evidence — the first time all three have pointed the same direction since the setup was built. USD/CAD takes a third consecutive downgrade to Low on the same accumulating logic in reverse: cooling momentum, an unresolved central bank question, and a live commodity headwind that hasn't let up. NZD/USD holds steady, neither improving nor deteriorating.

Next week is the real test for everything in this brief. FOMC, BoJ, and US Non-Farm Payrolls all land within three days, on top of whatever the weekend's chokepoint risk produces. This week's relative calm on the calendar should not be mistaken for the market's actual state.
