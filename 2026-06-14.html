The Iran-US Deal — What Actually Happened and What the Analysis Shows
What the Headlines Say
Pakistan PM Shehbaz Sharif confirmed Saturday: "Final, agreed upon text of peace deal between Iran and US has been reached." Multiple news outlets confirmed the framework via Pakistani mediation. Markets opened Sunday with risk-on impulse — oil fell, equities futures bid, EUR/USD spiked slightly. DXY retreated toward 99.50-99.75. This is the most concrete Iran-US deal language since negotiations began.
Structural Assessment — Why the Caution is Warranted
Iran's non-negotiable conditions make any durable agreement structurally improbable without one side capitulating on core principles. The key sticking points are: (1) enrichment right is absolute — Iran will not accept zero enrichment; (2) all sanctions must be lifted simultaneously before nuclear concessions; (3) frozen assets must be fully released; (4) no missile or regional proxy negotiations; (5) high-enriched uranium must stay in Iran. Analysis conclusion: "If the US accepts these terms, they have lost the war and everything returns to the old situation with Iran having even greater leverage than before. Is it possible? Yes. Is it probable? No." The market should treat this as framework noise, not durable resolution. Hormuz structural status unchanged until verified physical reopening.
